How to Extract Emails & Contact Details From Any Website

The signals to look for, manual vs. automated methods, and how to do it responsibly at scale.

Whether you're enriching a CRM, building a B2B prospect list, or just trying to reach a company, the contact details are usually already public on the site — you just have to know where the page hides them. Here's how to find and extract them reliably.

Where contact details actually live in the HTML

  • mailto: links — the most reliable email signal; an <a href="mailto:..."> is an explicit, intended contact.
  • tel: links — the cleanest phone signal, free of the false positives you get from scraping raw text (dates, prices, IDs).
  • Footer & contact page — most sites concentrate email, phone, and address here.
  • Social profile links — LinkedIn, X/Twitter, Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, GitHub URLs in the header/footer.
  • Structured data — some sites expose an Organization schema with email and telephone fields.

Manual vs. automated

For one page, Ctrl/Cmd-F for "@" and check the footer. But the moment you have a list of domains — a conference attendee list, a directory, a competitor set — manual stops scaling. That's where a parser that pulls every mailto:, validates phone formats, and resolves social URLs in one pass saves hours.
